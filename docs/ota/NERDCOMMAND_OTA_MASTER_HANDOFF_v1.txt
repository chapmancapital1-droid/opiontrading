SECTION 5 — MONTE CARLO SPEC (NUMERICAL METHODS)
================================================================================

Risk-neutral GBM:
  dS = (r − q) S dt + σ S dW
Exact one-step terminal:
  S_T = S0 * exp( (μ − ½σ²)T + σ√T Z ),  Z ~ N(0,1)
European call payoff: max(S_T − K, 0); put: max(K − S_T, 0)
Price = e^{−rT} mean(payoffs)
std_err = e^{−rT} std(payoffs) / √N   (or on discounted samples)
Antithetic: pair Z and −Z when steps=1
Multi-step: cumsum of increments (path-dependent / charts)
Convergence: abs(MC − BS) → 0 as N → ∞

Also implemented in TypeScript: src/domain/montecarlo.ts
  runMonteCarlo (strategy P/L)
  monteCarloEuropean (pricing vs BS)
  simulateGbmPaths

================================================================================

6.1 Marketing courses often claim "adjustment turns losers to BE/profit".
    Real mechanics (ranked likelihood):
      1. Convert long option → debit spread (sell OTM wing)  ★★★★★
      2. Average down (dangerous — doubles risk)
      3. Roll forward to next DTE
      4. Hedge with opposite option (strangle/straddle)
      5. Butterfly / broken-wing recovery structures

6.2 Our coded behavior
  EntryFilter score 0–100:
    trend vs EMA20 (40), ORB / ATR momentum (up to 30), IV rank (up to 20),
    range liquidity (10). Trade only if score ≥ 70 and direction clear.
  AdjustmentEngine:
    loss ≥ 40% of premium → CONVERT_TO_DEBIT_SPREAD
    near expiry + loss → ROLL_FORWARD
    moderate loss + vega → HEDGE_OPPOSITE_WING
    +50% premium → TAKE_PARTIAL_PROFIT

  Metrics always include commissions + slippage params.
  DO NOT ship expectancy claims from synthetic alone.
