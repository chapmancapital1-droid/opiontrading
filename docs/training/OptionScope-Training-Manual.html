2. Options trading education

2.1 Calls and puts

ContractBuyer’s rightSeller’s obligation
CallBuy 100 shares at strike by expirationSell 100 shares at strike if assigned
PutSell 100 shares at strike by expirationBuy 100 shares at strike if assigned

Standard equity option multiplier = 100 shares. Buyers pay premium; sellers receive premium and take the other side’s risk. Short options can be assigned — plan for it.

2.2 Long vs short

PositionCapitalRisk shape
Long optionDebit (premium paid)Max loss ≈ premium (single long)
Short option (naked)Credit receivedCan be large / undefined

Empire default: long and defined-risk spreads first. Naked short is not a Seed tool.

2.3 Vertical spreads (workhorse)

StructureLegs (concept)BiasMax lossMax profit
Bull call debitLong lower call + short higher callBullishNet debitWidth − debit
Bear put debitLong higher put + short lower putBearishNet debitWidth − debit
Bull put creditShort higher put + long lower putMild bull / neutralWidth − creditNet credit
Bear call creditShort lower call + long higher callMild bear / neutralWidth − creditNet credit

2.4 Other structures you will see in the Lab

  • Iron condor — short OTM put vertical + short OTM call vertical (range thesis).
  • CSP / covered call — capital heavy; often Stage 1+ or when you already hold shares.
  • Money Press (put diagonal) — sell near-term higher-strike put, buy lower-strike longer put as protection. Flat-to-up thesis; weekly roll discipline. Personal method notes only — not a promise of results.
  • Straddle / strangle — volatility thesis; can be capital intensive; use Compare and model PoP carefully.

2.5 Greeks in one breath

GreekMeaning
Δ DeltaApprox. dollar sensitivity / share-equivalent. Not win probability.
Γ GammaHow delta changes as price moves.
Θ ThetaTime decay (per day in OptionScope position scale).
ν VegaSensitivity to implied volatility.
ρ RhoSensitivity to interest rates.

2.6 IV, trend, events

  • High IV — premium rich; credit structures often preferred by books (still need edge + risk).
  • Low IV — debit / long vol more common in textbooks.
  • Earnings / events — can explode IV and gap through strikes; companion brain may avoid event proximity.
  • Liquidity — wide bid/ask destroys theoretical edge; prefer tight markets.

4. Dashboard usage guide

Run the app: cd opiontrading → pnpm dev → http://localhost:3000

Your live data mode (as of last config read): Alpaca paper for market data + account sizing. Demo mode needs no keys.

4.1 Command — /dashboard

Purpose: Morning cockpit. See capital truth, seed badge, journal pulse, chart, market context — not a “buy now” button.
  • Confirm equity / seed mode ($500 / $1k / $5k) matches reality or paper.
  • Check LIVE vs PAPER honesty labels (no fake live day P/L).
  • Jump to Trade Lab, Journal, or Settings from ritual CTAs.
  • Use chart + symbol search to set the day’s focus ticker.

4.2 Trade Lab — /builder

Purpose: Build a structure, run math, get brain ranks, copy a Robinhood checklist.
  1. Enter ticker → Load live chain (or Use demo).
  2. Pick strategy template (verticals, condor, Money Press, etc.).
  3. Review legs table, net debit/credit, max P/L, break-evens.
  4. Read payoff chart, Greeks panel, model PoP/EV (Monte Carlo).
  5. Hit brain Recommend — may lock strikes/DTE from live chain.
  6. If contracts = 0, read zero-size coach (capital too small — not a bug).
  7. Scroll to Order checklist → Copy → enter manually in broker.
  8. Save plan to Journal before you fill.

4.3 Library — /library

Search ingested option books / strategy rules. Use for thesis language and book-backed context — not as a signal service.

4.4 Compare — /compare

Pick up to three structures under the same spot / IV / DTE. Table shows net cash, max P/L, break-evens, model PoP, model EV, payoff RoR. Demo marks unless you compare ideas already priced in Lab.

4.5 Journal — /journal

Process loop: plan → open → close with realized P/L. Checklist text should appear on plans saved from Trade Lab. This is how you calibrate forecast vs outcome (Phase 7 mindset).

4.6 Saved — /saved

Quick local bookmark of ideas (this browser). Reopen in Lab or log in Journal later.

4.7 Settings — /settings

  • Seed presets: $500 / $1k / $5k — drives brain sizing policy.
  • Robinhood import: paste or CSV from official export only. Never enter RH password.
  • Alpaca: paper keys size the brain to live paper equity (no orders).

4.8 Education — /education

In-app risk field manual. This PDF is the longer portable version for study offline.

6. Seed account reality & checklist discipline

  • Empire seed policy: ~0.5% target / 1% hard risk per trade; tight open-risk budget; CSP blocked as default at seed.
  • Growth-aggressive mode is not for Seed psychology.
  • Zero-size coach means “this 1-lot does not fit capital” — switch to cheaper defined-risk or paper larger names.
  • Checklist is the climax: copy → broker. The app never auto-sends orders.
