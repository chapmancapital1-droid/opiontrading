2. Risk per trade respected empire ceilings.  
3. Forecast vs outcome is written down.  
4. Over a **sample of many trades**, expectancy is positive **after** costs and mistakes.

# Part II — Options in plain English

## Chapter 4 — Calls, puts, premium, 100-share unit

| | Call | Put |
|--|------|-----|
| Buyer | Right to **buy** 100 shares at strike | Right to **sell** 100 shares at strike |
| Seller | Obligation to sell if assigned | Obligation to buy if assigned |

- Premium is the price of the contract (×100 for dollars).  
- **Defined risk** = you know max loss at entry (spreads with long protection).  
- **Undefined / large** risk = naked short options — **out of beginner core**.

## Chapter 5 — The four safer strategies (preview)

Full training: **06-FOUR-STRATEGIES-TRAINING.md**

1. **Bull put credit spread** — moderately bullish / sideways; sell put spread.  
2. **Bear call credit spread** — moderately bearish / sideways; sell call spread.  
3. **Iron condor** — range-bound; sell both wings with long hedges.  
4. **Bull call debit spread** — bullish defined-risk (pay debit; cap risk).

# Part V — Path to “making profits” without lying

## Chapter 9 — Expectancy, not lottery

A profitable operator optimizes:

\[
\text{Expectancy} \approx (\text{Win\%} \times \text{Avg win}) - (\text{Loss\%} \times \text{Avg loss})
\]

OptionScope helps with:
- **Structure selection** (rules + context)  
- **Honest max loss** (defined risk)  
- **Sizing** (empire ceilings)  
- **Calibration** (journal forecast vs outcome)  

It does **not** create edge from nothing. Edge comes from **your rules + market + execution + sample size**.

## Chapter 10 — 30-day beginner plan

**Graduation criteria (not time):**  
≥20 paper closes logged · checklist used 100% · no revenge size · size 0 respected.
