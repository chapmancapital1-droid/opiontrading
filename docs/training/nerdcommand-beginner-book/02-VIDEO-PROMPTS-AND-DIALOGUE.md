## EP 0 — Cold open (60–90s)

### Avatar prompt
Global brand lock; slightly leaning in; “welcome to the desk” energy.

### Dialogue (teacher → student)

**Teacher:**  
“I’m not here to sell you lottery tickets. I’m here to put a **desk** under your options curiosity. NerdCommand’s OptionScope is the second brain: scan, check bias, size risk, copy a checklist, journal. You still own the broker click.”

**Student (on-screen text or second voice):**  
“So this isn’t signals?”

**Teacher:**  
“Correct. Process. Seed capital first. Defined risk. If you want guaranteed money, leave now — it doesn’t exist. If you want operator habits, stay.”

### On-screen
Education page “Read this first” → fade to Command.

### End card
“Educational only · Not investment advice · No auto-trade”

## EP 1 — Hard laws (4 min)

### Avatar prompt
Caution variant; amber light.

### Dialogue

**Teacher:**  
“Five laws. One: OptionScope does not place trades. Two: no passwords, no auto-trade. Three: probability of profit is a model under assumptions. Four: delta is not PoP. Five: seed accounts don’t pretend they can cash-secure puts on expensive names.”

**Student:**  
“What if the model says 80%?”

**Teacher:**  
“Then you still know your **max loss in dollars**, and you still journal. Eighty percent models lose 20% of the time — and clusters hurt.”

### On-screen
Settings seed $500 · brain size 0 example if available · Education laws.

## EP 3 — Chain literacy (5 min)

### Dialogue

**Teacher:**  
“Load a liquid name. SPY or a liquid stock. See expirations. See bid and ask. Wide ask means the market is charging you tuition. Beginners start liquid.”

**Student:**  
“What about cheap tickers?”

**Teacher:**  
“Cheap can be a trap: spreads, gaps, assignment drama. Scanner’s price band exists so you don’t romanticize pennies.”

### On-screen
Trade Lab load chain · zoom strikes · show DEMO vs LIVE badge discussion.

## EP 4–7 — Strategy episodes (template)

Use for each of the four strategies (see 06 for full content).

### Structure
1. Whiteboard: payoff sketch (credit or debit).  
2. When / when-not (IV, trend, events).  
3. Scanner filter.  
4. Lab recommend / legs.  
5. Max loss calculation out loud.  
6. Checklist copy.  
7. Journal plan line.  
8. “Do not live-trade until paper is boring.”

### Sample dialogue (bull put credit)

**Teacher:**  
“Bull put credit: you sell a put and buy a lower put. Max loss is width minus credit — known at entry. We want elevated-to-neutral IV, up or sideways bias, no earnings landmine.”

**Student:**  
“Why not just sell a naked put?”

**Teacher:**  
“Because seed capital and sleep. Defined risk is how empires survive long enough to matter.”
