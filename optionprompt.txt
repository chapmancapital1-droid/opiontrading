You are a senior fintech product designer, quantitative developer, and full-stack TypeScript engineer.

Build a production-ready responsive website application called “OptionScope,” an educational options strategy calculator and trading companion designed for people who place options trades through Robinhood.

This is an analysis and decision-support application, not an autonomous trading bot. The MVP must not request a user’s Robinhood username, password, two-factor authentication code, or session token. It must not use unofficial or reverse-engineered Robinhood endpoints. It should calculate and explain a trade, then create a Robinhood-ready order checklist that the user can manually verify and enter into Robinhood.

PRODUCT GOAL

Help an options trader answer these questions before entering a trade:

1. What is my total debit, credit, collateral, and maximum capital at risk?
2. What are my maximum possible profit and maximum possible loss?
3. At what underlying prices will the strategy break even?
4. What is the estimated probability of making money?
5. What is the estimated probability of losing money?
6. What is the expected dollar profit or loss under the selected assumptions?
7. How will the position react to changes in stock price, time, and implied volatility?
8. What exact legs should I review and enter into Robinhood?
9. After closing the trade, how accurate was the original probability estimate?

The interface must clearly state that all probabilities are model-based estimates, not guarantees or personalized investment recommendations.

TARGET USER

The primary user trades options through Robinhood and understands basic calls and puts but may not fully understand multi-leg risk, implied volatility, time decay, assignment risk, or the assumptions behind probability-of-profit calculations.

MVP USER FLOW

1. The user searches for or manually enters an underlying ticker.
2. Display the underlying price, quote timestamp, data source, and whether the quote is real-time, delayed, or manually entered.
3. The user chooses a strategy template or creates a custom strategy.
4. The user selects each option leg:
   - Buy or sell
   - Call or put
   - Quantity
   - Strike price
   - Expiration date
   - Bid
   - Ask
   - Mark price
   - Expected fill price
   - Implied volatility
   - Contract multiplier, defaulting to 100
5. The user can also include an underlying-stock leg for strategies such as a covered call.
6. The app immediately calculates the trade’s risk and reward.
7. The user adjusts assumptions such as implied volatility, target date, interest rate, dividend yield, expected stock drift, and simulation count.
8. The app displays payoff charts, probability results, Greeks, and scenario analysis.
9. The user saves the analysis to a trade journal.
10. The app generates a copyable “Robinhood Order Checklist.”
11. Later, the user enters the actual opening fill, closing fill, and realized result to compare the forecast with the outcome.

SUPPORTED STRATEGIES

Include these strategy templates:

- Long call
- Long put
- Covered call
- Cash-secured put
- Bull call debit spread
- Bear put debit spread
- Bull put credit spread
- Bear call credit spread
- Long straddle
- Long strangle
- Iron condor
- Iron butterfly
- Call butterfly
- Put butterfly
- Custom same-expiration multi-leg strategy

Allow the user to select an options-permission profile. Hide or warn about strategies that may not be supported by that profile.

Do not permit an uncovered short call or uncovered short put in the default Robinhood companion mode. A separate educational sandbox may model undefined-risk positions, but it must display a prominent undefined-risk warning.

Keep calendar spreads and other strategies with multiple expiration dates out of the first MVP. Add them in a later phase because calculating their risk requires valuing remaining legs at an intermediate expiration.

CALCULATION ENGINE

Create a tested calculation engine separate from the presentation layer.

For every leg, store:

- Underlying symbol
- Asset type: option or stock
- Position side: long or short
- Option type: call or put
- Number of contracts
- Strike price
- Expiration date and time
- Premium per share
- Contract multiplier
- Implied volatility
- Exercise style
- Fees
- Quote timestamp

Expiration profit and loss formulas:

Long call:
max(underlying price - strike, 0) × contracts × multiplier
minus premium paid and fees

Short call:
premium received
minus max(underlying price - strike, 0) × contracts × multiplier
minus fees

Long put:
max(strike - underlying price, 0) × contracts × multiplier
minus premium paid and fees

Short put:
premium received
minus max(strike - underlying price, 0) × contracts × multiplier
minus fees

Stock leg:
(target stock price - stock entry price) × number of shares

The total strategy profit or loss is the sum of all legs after premiums and fees.

Calculate and display:

- Net debit or net credit per share
- Total debit or credit in dollars
- Estimated collateral
- Maximum profit
- Maximum loss
- Whether the risk or reward is unlimited or undefined
- All break-even prices
- Return on risk
- Profit and loss at the current stock price
- Aggregate delta
- Aggregate gamma
- Aggregate theta
- Aggregate vega
- Probability of profit
- Probability of loss
- Probability of maximum profit, when applicable
- Probability of maximum loss, when applicable
- Expected profit or loss
- Median simulated result
- Fifth-percentile result
- Ninety-fifth-percentile result

Do not determine maximum profit or loss by looking only at the visible chart range. Analyze the payoff function at all strike breakpoints and at both price-distribution tails. Correctly identify unlimited risk or reward.

Find break-even prices by detecting roots in the complete net payoff function. Use a reliable numerical method such as interval scanning followed by bisection.

PROBABILITY MODES

Provide two clearly labeled probability modes.

Mode 1: Market-Implied Probability

- Use a risk-neutral price distribution.
- Use the risk-free rate minus dividend yield as the risk-neutral drift.
- Use an appropriate expected volatility input, preferably at-the-money implied volatility for the selected expiration.
- Explain that this is a market-implied modeling view and is not a forecast of actual returns.

Mode 2: User-Assumption Probability

- Allow the user to enter an expected annual return or drift.
- Allow the user to use zero drift, a manually entered view, or an optional historical estimate.
- Clearly label any historical estimate and show the lookback period.
- Explain that changing the expected return can materially change the probability result.

Use Monte Carlo simulation with:

- 20,000 simulations by default
- User-selectable simulation count
- A reproducible random seed
- Geometric Brownian motion for the MVP
- Fixed-volatility assumptions for the MVP
- A clear statement that volatility may change in real markets
- Monte Carlo standard error or a confidence interval for the estimated probability

Define:

Probability of profit =
number of simulations with strategy profit greater than zero
divided by total valid simulations

Probability of loss =
number of simulations with strategy profit less than zero
divided by total valid simulations

Do not describe option delta as the exact probability of profit. It may be shown as a separate market sensitivity metric.

OPTION VALUATION

At expiration, calculate intrinsic payoff directly.

For target dates before expiration:

- Reprice every remaining option at the selected target date.
- Select the valuation model from contract metadata.
- Support Bjerksund–Stensland or a tested binomial model for American-style contracts.
- Support Black–Scholes for European-style contracts.
- Include interest rate and dividend yield.
- Allow implied volatility to remain unchanged, increase, decrease, or follow a user-defined scenario.
- Clearly display which model and assumptions were used.

Use decimal-safe money calculations. Avoid floating-point display errors in premiums, credits, and total dollar results.

   - Let the user select a date before expiration

   - Rows represent possible underlying prices
   - Columns represent dates
   - Cells show estimated strategy profit or loss

   - Mark zero profit, expected value, median, and major percentiles

Important safeguards added

The companion document does not blindly treat every statement in the references as a calculation rule. It specifically prevents the app from:

Describing option selling as guaranteed, safe, passive, or non-losing
Treating delta as the exact probability of profit
Assuming an option expiring worthless means the entire position was profitable
Showing a new rolling credit while hiding the realized loss on the original option
Calling covered-call premium complete downside protection
Selecting a “best” strategy without explaining risk and assumptions
Copying source artwork, promotional claims, testimonials, or extended passages into the public app

I also noted that the uploaded 69-page book ends during its iron-condor section even though its contents page lists later chapters on managing, closing, and tracking positions.
